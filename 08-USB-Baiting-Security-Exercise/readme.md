# USB Baiting (Parking Lot Attack) Analysis

## Overview

This exercise examines a common social engineering technique known as a **USB baiting attack** (also called a *Parking Lot Attack*). The objective was to evaluate the risks associated with unknown removable media and recommend security controls to prevent compromise.

---

## Objective

- Identify sensitive information stored on removable media.
- Analyze how attackers could exploit discovered information.
- Assess organizational risks.
- Recommend preventive security controls.

---

## Scenario

An employee discovers a USB drive left in a public location and examines its contents.

The exercise involves evaluating the information found on the device, considering how an attacker could misuse it, and proposing safeguards to reduce the likelihood and impact of similar attacks.

---

## Security Concepts

- Social Engineering
- USB Baiting Attack
- Physical Security

---

## Findings

The USB device contained:

- Personally identifiable information (PII)
- Employee work-related documents
- Organizational operational information

Exposure of this information could assist attackers in gathering intelligence for future attacks.

---

## Risk Analysis

Information stored on removable media can be leveraged to:

- Conduct targeted phishing campaigns.
- Perform social engineering attacks.
- Gather intelligence about employees.
- Facilitate unauthorized access attempts.
- Increase the likelihood of malware infections if the USB contains malicious software.

---

## Recommended Security Controls

### Technical Controls

- Disable USB AutoRun/AutoPlay.
- Deploy endpoint protection and antivirus solutions.
- Restrict unauthorized USB device usage through endpoint management policies.

### Operational Controls

- Perform regular antivirus scans.
- Monitor endpoint activity for unauthorized removable media usage.
- Establish procedures for reporting suspicious devices.

### Managerial Controls

- Conduct regular security awareness training.
- Educate employees about USB baiting and social engineering.
- Define organizational policies for handling unknown removable media.

## Key Takeaways

Removable media can serve as an effective attack vector when combined with social engineering techniques.

Organizations should combine technical safeguards, administrative policies, and employee awareness programs to reduce the risk of USB-based attacks and protect sensitive organizational information.
