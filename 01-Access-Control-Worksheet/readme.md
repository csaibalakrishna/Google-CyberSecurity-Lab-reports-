# Access Control Worksheet

## Overview

This lab focuses on identifying authorization weaknesses within an organization's access control process and recommending appropriate security measures to reduce the risk of unauthorized access.

---

## Objective

Analyze an access control scenario to:

- Identify security issues related to authentication and authorization.
- Determine potential security risks.
- Recommend security controls based on industry best practices.

---

## Scenario

A review of user account activity revealed that a contractor continued to have access to the organization's payroll system even after their contract had ended.

The exercise required analyzing the available information, identifying authorization issues, and proposing security recommendations to prevent similar incidents.

---

## Security Concepts

- Authentication
- Authorization
- Identity and Access Management (IAM)
- Principle of Least Privilege (PoLP)
- Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)
- User Account Management
- Access Reviews

---

## Findings

The following security issues were identified:

- A former contractor's account remained active after the contract ended.
- The user retained unnecessary access to sensitive business resources.
- The organization lacked proper account deprovisioning procedures.

---

## Risk Analysis

If inactive accounts remain enabled, attackers or former employees may gain unauthorized access to confidential systems and data.

Potential impacts include:

- Unauthorized access to payroll information
- Data breaches
- Insider threats
- Violation of organizational security policies

---

## Recommendations

The following controls help mitigate the identified risks:

- Disable user accounts immediately after employment or contracts end.
- Implement the Principle of Least Privilege (PoLP).
- Perform periodic access reviews and permission audits.
- Enable Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for sensitive systems.
- Maintain an automated user provisioning and deprovisioning process.

---

## Key Takeaways

This exercise reinforced the importance of proper access control management throughout the user lifecycle.

Effective Identity and Access Management (IAM) practices reduce the attack surface by ensuring users receive only the permissions required for their roles and that obsolete accounts are removed promptly.
